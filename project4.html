Statistical Analysis & Hypothesis Testing

Is Tesla a tech company or a traditional automaker? This study applies statistical testing to explore Tesla’s identity through return behaviors compared to industry peers.

Project Overview

This project was completed at Columbia University for IEOR E4150 with Professor Dr. A. B. Dieker. It applies a series of statistical tests and regressions to assess whether Tesla behaves more like a tech stock or an automaker.

Dataset

The dataset includes daily open and close stock prices from 2018-11-27 to 2020-11-25 for 9 companies across the automotive and technology sectors, plus the NASDAQ index. Log returns were used for the analysis.

  • Automotive: Tesla (TSLA), Daimler (DAI.DE), Toyota (TM), GM, Ford (F)
  • Technology: Apple (AAPL), Google (GOOG), Dell, Microsoft (MSFT)
  • Index: NASDAQ Composite (^IXIC)

Methodology

We performed individual studies of log-returns for each company, tested distribution normality, estimated parameters with confidence intervals, and conducted regression analyses to explore industry behavior patterns.

Tesla showed notably higher volatility compared to its peers, particularly during the 2020 COVID-19 market disruptions. Confidence intervals suggested higher variance in tech companies and Tesla.

Linear regression showed slopes close to zero, indicating time alone doesn't explain returns. However, Tesla’s pattern deviated from traditional automakers.

Pairwise & Multiple Regressions

We conducted hypothesis tests comparing Tesla with each company, both with unequal variances and paired t-tests. Tesla’s strongest similarities were with tech firms like Apple and Google.

Multiple regression: Tesla vs Tech had R² = 0.15 vs R² = 0.11 for Tesla vs Automakers, indicating Tesla aligns more with tech sector behavior.

Conclusion

This statistical study suggests Tesla is not a typical car manufacturer. It shares more behavioral traits with technology companies. While complex factors influence stock returns, Tesla’s deviation from automotive norms is statistically evident and warrants deeper exploration.
