Crypto News Sentiment Analysis using NLP

Analyzed Bitcoin and Ethereum news headlines using natural language processing (NLP) to evaluate sentiment trends, highlight key entities, and understand public perception across cryptocurrencies.

Project Overview

This project applied a suite of NLP techniques to real-time news headlines related to Bitcoin and Ethereum. Using sentiment scoring, token frequency, and named entity recognition, I extracted insights about how each coin was being portrayed in the media. The analysis was performed with Python using libraries such as vaderSentiment, NLTK, and spaCy, and visualized through word clouds and frequency charts.

1. Sentiment Analysis Results

Using the VADER sentiment analyzer, I computed polarity scores for dozens of articles per coin. The key findings are summarized below:

  • Highest mean positive score: Ethereum (0.0667)
  • Highest individual negative score: Bitcoin (0.10)
  • Highest individual positive score: Ethereum (0.173)
  • Mean negative scores: Bitcoin – 0.0148, Ethereum – 0.0203

2. Natural Language Processing

Each article was tokenized, and stop words were removed. I generated n-grams and calculated word frequencies to identify the most common terms associated with each cryptocurrency.

Most Frequent Words

Bitcoin: price, bitcoin, crypto, digital, currency, market, blockchain...

Ethereum: ethereum, blockchain, defi, price, crypto, platform...

Word Clouds

Word clouds were generated to visualize the most prominent words in the headlines for each coin.

3. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

Using spaCy’s NER pipeline, I identified key organizations and entities mentioned alongside each coin. These included companies, financial institutions, and influential individuals.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Natural Language Processing can be used to understand crypto market sentiment through news media. Ethereum showed a slightly more positive tone across sources, while Bitcoin headlines exhibited more extreme polarity. These tools provide a scalable way to measure public perception and could be extended into more complex trading signal models based on news data.
