Customer Analytics: Segmentation, Engagement & Campaign Response

This project applied customer segmentation, engagement tracking, and predictive modeling to identify key drivers of marketing campaign success.

Goal: Build actionable customer insights using segmentation, engagement metrics, and predictive modeling to guide targeting and retention strategy.

Approach

  • Data cleaning & preparation (null handling, outlier detection, column standardization, engineered IDs).
  • Feature engineering: created Spend_Total including gold products, campaign conversion rates, and re-engagement KPIs (30/60/90 days).
  • Customer segmentation: RFM analysis in SQL, mapped into labeled segments (“Champions,” “At Risk”).
  • Predictive modeling: Random Forest and linear regression to evaluate campaign response and total spend.
  • Model interpretation: feature importance ranking, partial dependence plots, and transformations for skewed data.

Full EDA and detailed data preparation steps are available in the GitHub README.

Results

  • Customer engagement: Over half of customers (58%) re-engaged within 90 days, with nearly one-third returning in just 30 days—showing strong retention among key groups.
  • Campaign effectiveness: While average conversion hovered around 7%, the most recent campaign stood out with a 15% success rate, highlighting momentum in campaign performance.
  • Segmentation insights: RFM analysis revealed ~20% high-value “Champions” driving most of the revenue, while ~35% fell into “At Risk” categories, representing an opportunity for reactivation strategies.
  • Key drivers: Machine learning showed that online behavior (Purchases_Web), household income, and family structure (Kids_Count) were among the strongest predictors of campaign response.
  • Spend behavior: Regression confirmed that customers with higher online activity and higher incomes consistently spent more, reinforcing the value of digital-first targeting.

Key Takeaways

  • Segmentation clearly separated profitable “Champions” from disengaged customers needing recovery efforts.
  • Campaign performance improved over time, with the last initiative doubling average conversion.
  • Digital engagement and income emerged as actionable levers for targeting and personalized outreach.
