Supply Chain Modeling

Our deep learning model based on supply chain analysis significantly outperformed traditional forecasting methods, achieving a 0.9872 correlation with actual stock prices.

This project explores how the systematic study of businesses within a company's supply chain can help predict its stock price. Using Intel Corp as the target, we trained both a neural network and a regression model using stock prices of Intel’s suppliers, distributors, and clients.

Supply Chain Diagram

The idea draws from the understanding that supply chains are interconnected networks. If key clients struggle, it can signal trouble upstream. Conversely, performance issues from critical suppliers can hinder production.

Model Architecture

A neural network was trained with data from companies in Intel’s supply chain (e.g., Applied Materials, TSMC, Dell, HP) and compared with a model trained on Intel’s competitors (e.g., AMD, NVIDIA, Qualcomm).

Model Training

Hyperparameters were fine-tuned manually to reduce error without overfitting. Training performance was evaluated with K-fold validation and tracked using MAE and loss across epochs.

Results: Supply Chain vs Competition

The supply chain model achieved a correlation of 0.9872 with real stock prices using deep learning, outperforming both the competitor-based model and a traditional regression model.

Sample Results

These results suggest that incorporating supply chain insights into equity forecasting offers a promising new approach for financial modeling and investment analysis.
