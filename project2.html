Supply Chain Modeling

Forecasting Intel’s stock price using machine learning and supply chain data.

The goal of this project is to explore the idea that the systematic study of the movement in prices of businesses that comprise the supply chain of a certain company can lead to predicting the stock's price of this very company.

We propose building machine learning models where the features are the stock prices of supply chain partners of a company. As a case study, Intel was chosen as the target. The approach compares two models: one using data from Intel’s supply chain (suppliers, distributors, clients) and another using competitors’ stock prices.

Supply Chain Used:

  • Suppliers: Applied Materials, TSMC
  • Distributors: Arrow Corp, Avnet, Synnex
  • Clients: Dell, HP, Lenovo

Comparison Model:

  • Competitors: AMD, NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Broadcom, Micron, Texas Instruments

Both models were trained using historical price data. The deep learning model outperformed a traditional ML regression model.
