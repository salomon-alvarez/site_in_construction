Customer Analytics: Segmentation, Engagement & Campaign Response

This project applied customer segmentation, engagement tracking, and predictive modeling to identify key drivers of marketing campaign success.

Goal: Build actionable customer insights using segmentation, engagement metrics, and predictive modeling to guide targeting and retention strategy.

Approach

  • Data cleaning & preparation (null handling, outlier detection, column standardization, engineered IDs).
  • Feature engineering: created Spend_Total including gold products, campaign conversion rates, and re-engagement KPIs (30/60/90 days).
  • Customer segmentation: RFM analysis in SQL, mapped into labeled segments (“Champions,” “At Risk”).
  • Predictive modeling: Random Forest and linear regression to evaluate campaign response and total spend.
  • Model interpretation: feature importance ranking, partial dependence plots, and transformations for skewed data.

Full EDA and detailed data preparation steps are available in the GitHub README.

Results

  • Engagement: 32% of customers re-engaged within 30 days; 58% within 90 days.
  • Campaigns: Conversion averaged 7%, with the last campaign reaching 15%.
  • Segmentation: RFM identified ~20% “Champions,” ~35% “At Risk.”
  • Predictors: Random Forest ranked Purchases_Web, Income, and Kids_Count among the strongest campaign response drivers.
  • Regression: Significant predictors included online purchase behavior and income.

Key Takeaways

  • Segmentation clearly separated high-value “Champions” from disengaged customers.
  • Campaign conversion varied widely; recent campaigns showed strongest impact.
  • Engagement metrics and predictive modeling highlighted actionable drivers for targeting.
